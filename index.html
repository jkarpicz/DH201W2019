Created in 1974, the Washington Art in Public Places Program is the second oldest state percent for the art program in the United States. Over 45 years, the collection has grown to include over 4,700 pieces, 882 of which (~19%) are housed at Washington's public colleges and universities. This project explores where 'who' and 'why' questions primarily: exploring the artists and campus sites that comprise state's collection. The project explores these pieces of art within the context of scholarship on public art, campus development, and higher education. It will narrate a brief historical context of percent for arts programs nationally and locally in the state of Washington, as well as include information about the organization of Washington's Art in Public Places program.
